This week, an escalated SOC Level II ticket landed on my desk: a login attempt from an IP with 100% confidence of abuse and a very poor reputation. The user entered the correct credentials but could not pass MFA, raising the risk of a potential compromised account.  
I conducted a deep investigation using Microsoft Entra ID sign-in and audit logs, Splunk SPL queries, VirusTotal, abused IP databases, and threat intel feeds. Actions taken included resetting credentials, blocking the malicious IP, and implementing ongoing monitoring. This case highlights the critical importance of SOC Level II analysis, where connecting multiple signals can prevent serious security incidents.
